About Bipolar I Disorder

Manic Episodes

Characterized by elevated mood, increased energy, racing thoughts, and impulsive behavior. Individuals may experience feelings of euphoria, grandiosity, decreased need for sleep, and heightened creativity. These episodes can significantly impact decision-making and social relationships, lasting at least one week or requiring hospitalization.

Depressive Episodes

Marked by persistent sadness, loss of interest in activities, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, and feelings of worthlessness or hopelessness. These episodes can severely impact daily functioning, work performance, and personal relationships, often lasting weeks or months without treatment.

Mixed Episodes

Characterized by experiencing symptoms of both mania and depression simultaneously or in rapid alternation. Individuals may feel agitated with racing thoughts while also experiencing profound sadness or emptiness. This state can be particularly challenging and may increase risk of harmful behaviors due to the combination of high energy and negative emotions.

About This Artwork

This interactive digital experience aims to provide insight into the dramatic emotional and perceptual shifts experienced in Bipolar I Disorder. Through color transitions, ambient animations, and rotating thoughts, the artwork creates an immersive representation of these contrasting states.

The statistics tracker allows you to observe how time is spent in each state, reflecting the cyclical nature of the condition. The optional audio integration provides ambient soundscapes that complement each emotional state.
